Welcome to Antarctica

Historical and Cultural Highlights

Antarctica is Earth's southernmost continent, covered in ice and home to some of the most extreme environments on the planet. Although largely uninhabited, it plays a vital role in scientific research and environmental studies.

Its discovery dates back to the early 19th century, and it has since been the subject of numerous explorations. Today, Antarctica serves as a key location for international scientific research, focusing on climate change, glaciology, and marine biology.
